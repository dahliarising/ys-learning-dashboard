À:
martin@renault.fr

Responsable Technique
Engineering Department

Key Corrections

  • ✗ "Je demande vous" → "Je vous prie de" (Asking with respect)
  • ✗ "Veuillez trouver joint" → "Veuillez trouver ci-joint" (Complete formula)
  • ✗ "Je suis très besoin" → "J'aurais besoin" (Conditional politeness)
  • ✗ "À bientôt," → "Cordialement," (Professional register)
  • ✗ Subject line should be specific and detailed
  • ✗ Use "Monsieur/Madame" instead of first names in formal email
  • ✗ Sign with title and department at the office

Objet:

Je vous remercie d'avance,
Ingénieur Park
Département Suspension

Key Corrections

  • ✗ "Je besoin à clarifier" → "J'aurais besoin de clarifier" (Subjunctive needed)
  • ✗ "une point" → "un point" (False friend: "point" is masculine)
  • ✗ "Est-ce que vous pouvez" → "Pouvez-vous" (Too casual/indirect)
  • ✗ "C'est urgent!" → "Cela demande une réponse rapide." (Professional tone)
  • ✗ "Merci beaucoup!" → "Je vous remercie d'avance," (Formal register)
  • ✗ No department or title in signature

Formal Email Structure

격식 이메일 구조

Complete business email template

Professional Email Structure

OBJET LINE
Objet: [Specific topic] — [Department/Project name]
Example: "Livraison de composants — Projet K-SUV"
GREETING
Monsieur [Nom], or Madame [Nom],
NEVER: "Martin" or "Bonjour Martin" in formal context
OPENING STATEMENT
Je vous prie de [+infinitive] or Je vous remercie de [+infinitive]
Opens email with respect and structure
MAIN CONTENT
• Use complete sentences (not sentence fragments)
• Vous form consistently
• Clear paragraph breaks (1-2 topics per paragraph)
• Attachment notation: Veuillez trouver ci-joint le document
CLOSING STATEMENT
Je vous remercie d'avance, or À votre convenance,
Professional and polite
SIGN-OFF
Cordialement,
[Full Name]
[Title]
[Department]

Key Takeaways

핵심 요약

3 Critical Email Formulas

FORMULA 1
Je vous prie de [infinitive]
Formal requests. Use in emails.
FORMULA 2
Veuillez trouver ci-joint [noun]
Attachment notification. Complete formula.
FORMULA 3
Je vous remercie de [noun/gerund]
Formal thanks. Always with "de", not "à".

Pre-Send Checklist

  • ▢ Subject line is specific and includes project/department
  • ▢ Greeting uses "Monsieur/Madame [Name]"
  • ▢ Email opens with "Je vous prie de" or "Je vous remercie de"
  • ▢ Attachments labeled: "Veuillez trouver ci-joint"
  • ▢ Vous form used consistently throughout
  • ▢ Closing uses "Cordialement," or "À votre convenance,"
  • ▢ Signature includes title, department, and company affiliation
  • ▢ No colloquial expressions ("à bientôt", "super", "cool")
  • ▢ No excessive exclamation marks or overused adjectives
  • ▢ Grammar and spelling checked (use French spellchecker)
