Dialogue: Suspension Development Meeting at Technocentre

🎧 Shadowing Mode (섀도우잉 모드)
Listen to the entire dialogue in French at native speed (fr-FR voice), then repeat each line after a pause. This builds natural pronunciation and comprehension. Start with the full dialogue, then focus on individual exchanges.
Ingénieur FR (Laurent):
"Commençons la réunion. L'ordre du jour est la validation du nouveau système de suspension pour la K5."
(회의를 시작합시다. 오늘 의제는 K5용 신형 서스펜션 시스템 검증입니다.)
Vous (엔지니어 KR):
"Merci de votre présence. Je voudrais d'abord présenter nos résultats des tests de durabilité."
(참석해주셔서 감사합니다. 먼저 내구성 시험 결과를 제시하고 싶습니다.)
Laurent:
"Excellent. Pourriez-vous clarifier les spécifications du ressort avant d'entrer dans les détails?"
(훌륭합니다. 세부 사항에 들어가기 전에 스프링 사양을 명확히 해주실 수 있을까요?)
Vous:
"À mon avis, la raideur du ressort doit être augmentée de 8%. Si je peux me permettre, je sugère une modification du design."
(제 생각에는 스프링 강도를 8% 증가시켜야 합니다. 제가 감히 말씀드리자면, 디자인 수정을 제안합니다.)
Laurent:
"Pourrais-je poser une question? Avez-vous considéré l'impact sur le confort de conduite?"
(질문을 해도 될까요? 운전 편안함에 미치는 영향을 고려했습니까?)
Vous:
"Je suis d'accord. Nos simulations indiquent une amélioration de 12% de la stabilité avec une réduction de 3% du confort."
(동의합니다. 우리 시뮬레이션은 안정성이 12% 개선되고 편안함이 3% 감소함을 나타냅니다.)
Laurent:
"C'est acceptable. Pour résumer, nous approuvons la modification. Les prochaines étapes sont les tests en piste."
(괜찮습니다. 요약하자면, 수정을 승인합니다. 다음 단계는 주행 테스트입니다.)
Vous:
"Qui est responsable de coordonner ces tests? La date limite est le 30 avril."
(누가 이 테스트를 조정할 책임이 있습니까? 마감일은 4월 30일입니다.)
Laurent:
"Je m'en charge avec l'équipe de validation. Merci à tous, bonne journée."
(검증 팀과 함께 책임지겠습니다. 모두 감사합니다, 좋은 하루 되세요.)

Dialogue Review

📋 Understanding Check
Q1: What is the main topic of the meeting?
A: Validation of a new suspension system for the K5 model. (K5용 신형 서스펜션 시스템 검증)
Q2: What modification does the Korean engineer suggest?
A: Increase spring stiffness by 8% and modify the design. (스프링 강도를 8% 증가시키고 디자인을 수정)
Q3: What are the next steps after the meeting?
A: Track testing (tests en piste) with a deadline of April 30th. (4월 30일 마감일로 주행 테스트)
Q4: What polite expressions were used to present opinions?
A: "À mon avis..." (In my opinion), "Si je peux me permettre..." (If I may), "Je voudrais..." (I would like). (의견 제시 표현)

Grammar: Conditional for Politeness

📊 Conditional Tense Structure (조건문)
🎯 Meeting-Specific Conditional Uses
Pattern Example Usage
Je voudrais + infinitive Je voudrais ajouter un point Polite contribution
Pourriez-vous + infinitive Pourriez-vous clarifier ce point? Polite question
Je suggérerais que + subjunctive Je suggérerais qu'on augmente la raideur Gentle proposal
Si je peux me permettre... Si je peux me permettre, je suggère... Very humble opinion
💡 Key Rules
✓ Conditional = Polite + Formal in professional contexts. Always use it in meetings instead of imperative.
✓ Never use "tu" forms in business meetings. Stick with "vous" conditional forms.
✓ "Si je peux me permettre..." shows extreme politeness before expressing disagreement or alternative ideas.
✓ "Je suis d'accord" (I agree) is neutral formal agreement. No conditional needed here.

Pronunciation Drills

🎯 Drill 1: Opening with Nasal Vowels
Expression: "Commençons la réunion" [kɔmɑ̃sɔ̃ la ʁeynjɔ̃]
Korean guide: 꼼앙송 라 흐외니용
Focus: [ɑ̃] (앙), [ɔ̃] (옹), /ʁ/ (흐) - Don't use /r/ sound, use guttural /ʁ/
Repeat: 3 times slowly, then 3 times naturally
🎯 Drill 2: Polite Question Pattern
Expression: "Pourriez-vous clarifier ce point?" [puʁje vu klaʁifje sə pwɛ̃]
Korean guide: 푸흐이에 부 클라흐피에 스 뿌앙
Focus: [pwɛ̃] (뿌앙 - /w/ + nasal /ɛ̃/), final /ʁ/ in "clarifier"
Repeat: Focus on linking "vous" → "clarifier" smoothly
🎯 Drill 3: Agreement Expression
Expression: "Je suis d'accord" [ʒə sɥi dakɔʁ]
Korean guide: 쥐 스위 다꼬흐
Focus: [sɥi] (스위 - /s/ + /ɥ/), [ɔʁ] (오흐 - rounded vowel + guttural)
Repeat: 5 times to master the /ɥ/ sound (like English "sweet" but with lips rounded)
🎯 Drill 4: Humble Opinion
Expression: "Si je peux me permettre" [si ʒə pø mə pɛʁmɛtʁ]
Korean guide: 시 쥐 푀 므 페흐메트흐
Focus: [pø] (푀 - /ø/ rounded vowel), [pɛʁ] (페흐), French /ʁ/
Repeat: Practice slow, then normal speed. Pay attention to linking.
🎯 Drill 5: Closing Summary
Expression: "Pour résumer, les prochaines étapes" [puʁ rezyme lɛ pʁɔʃɛn etap]
Korean guide: 푸흐 흐외주메 레 프흐로셰느 에탑
Focus: [ʁe] (흐외), [pʁɔ] (프흐로), nasal /ɛ̃/ in "prochaines"
Repeat: Practice the /pʁ/ cluster and the liaison between words

Knowledge Check Quiz

Question 1: Polite Opening
How do you politely start a meeting?
✓ Correct! "Commençons la réunion" is the proper professional opening (회의를 시작합시다).
Question 2: Asking for Clarification
Which expression is best for asking a colleague to clarify a technical point?
✓ Correct! "Pourriez-vous clarifier ce point?" uses the conditional for politeness (명확히 해주시겠어요?).
Question 3: Expressing Agreement
How do you formally agree with a proposal in a meeting?
✓ Correct! "Je suis d'accord" is formal and appropriate for professional meetings (동의합니다).
Question 4: Humble Suggestion
What phrase precedes a gentle disagreement or alternative suggestion?
✓ Correct! "Si je peux me permettre..." shows extreme politeness before respectfully disagreeing (제가 감히 말씀드리자면).
Question 5: Closing Statement
How would you introduce the next steps after summarizing?
✓ Correct! "Les prochaines étapes sont..." is professional closing language (다음 단계는...).
